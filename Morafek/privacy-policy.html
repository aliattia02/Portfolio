Privacy Policy

CareMate by Clida.Tech · Last updated: July 14, 2026

CareMate ("we", "us", "our") is a FHIR‑native care coordination platform built for the German healthcare market. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, and how it is protected. This project is a demo/portfolio application and is not intended to replace a certified production healthcare system.

1. Data We Collect

  • Account data: name, email address, role (patient or clinician), and authentication credentials.
  • Clinical data: vital signs (heart rate, SpO₂, glucose, blood pressure), encounters, conditions, and diagnosis codes (ICD‑10‑GM).
  • Documents: clinical documents and files uploaded via the platform.
  • Communications: secure messages exchanged between patients and clinicians.
  • Technical data: device identifiers, IP address, and log data used for security and troubleshooting.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • To provide core functionality: care coordination, vitals monitoring, and clinical documentation.
  • To support AI‑assisted clinical coding suggestions (processed via Gemini AI).
  • To maintain security, prevent abuse, and comply with legal obligations.
  • To generate exportable FHIR Bundles for patient record portability.

3. Legal Basis & DSGVO / GDPR Compliance

Where the platform processes data of individuals in Germany or the EU, we rely on explicit patient consent and legitimate interest in providing healthcare coordination services, in line with the DSGVO (German GDPR implementation).

Your rights under DSGVO/GDPR include:

  • Right to access your personal and clinical data.
  • Right to rectification of inaccurate data.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten").
  • Right to data portability (via FHIR Bundle export).
  • Right to withdraw consent at any time.

4. Data Storage & Security

  • Authentication tokens (JWT) are stored in secure mobile keystores.
  • Clinical documents are stored via Cloudinary with LOINC‑categorised access controls.
  • Access to patient data is role‑based (RBAC), limited to authorized clinicians.
  • An offline queue may temporarily store data locally on‑device during connectivity gaps, then sync securely once online.

5. Third‑Party Services

We use limited third‑party services to operate the platform, including cloud document storage (Cloudinary) and AI‑assisted coding suggestions (Google Gemini). These providers process data only as necessary to deliver their respective services and are not permitted to use your data for other purposes.

6. Data Retention

Clinical and account data is retained only as long as necessary to provide the service or as required by applicable law. You may request deletion of your data at any time, subject to any legal retention requirements for healthcare records.

To request deletion of your account and associated data, visit our Account Deletion page, which explains the steps to request deletion, what data is deleted, and any retention periods that apply.

7. Children's Privacy

CareMate is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from minors without appropriate parental or guardian consent, in accordance with applicable healthcare and data protection regulations.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

9. Contact

For questions about this Privacy Policy or to exercise your data rights, please reach out via the contact section of Clida.Tech.
